Milly Alcock (born on April 11, 2000) is a 22 years old Australian actress who is in the news these days because of her performance as Princess Rhaenyra Targaryen in “House of the Dragon.” It is a Miguel Sapochnik-directed “Game of Thrones” spinoff involving artists like Paddy Considine, Olivia Cooke, Emma D’Arcy, and Matt Smith.

The role of “Princess Rainera Targaryen” changed her life as she had undoubtedly worked on several projects before getting a job in “House of the Dragon,” but for some reason, she had to work in a restaurant, even in an interview with Sunday’s Stellar Milly’s stated that “I never thought this would happen to me. I was washing dishes in a restaurant, living in my mum’s attic. This doesn’t happen to people like me, so it was incredibly quick.”

House of the Dragon is not Alcock’s first show, as, before this, she has worked on many popular shows involving “Pine Gap,” “Reckoning,” “A Place to Call Home,” and “Les Norton.” She has also featured in movies like “The School” and “The Familiars.” She was 18 when the Casting Guild of Australia chose her for the Rising Stars Award.
