Neal H. Moritz Net Worth

What is Neal H. Moritz's Net Worth?

Neal H. Moritz is an American film and television producer who has a net worth of $150 million. As of this writing, Neal is the fifth highest-grossing producer in film history. His movies have grossed $9.5 billion at the global box office, not including DVD sales, merchandise or home box office. His highest-grossing film to date is Furious 7 which earned $1.7 billion globally.

Neal H. Moritz was born in Los Angeles, California in June 1959. He executive produced the television series Tru Calling from 2003 to 2005. From 2005 to 2006 he served as executive producer for the TV series Point Pleasant. Moritz executive produced the series Prison break from 2005 to 2009 and the series The Big C from 2010 to 2012. In 2013 he executive produced the TV series Save Me. He has also produced the movies Juice, The Stoned Age, Volcano, I Know What You Did Last Summer, Urban Legend, I Still Know What You Did Last Summer, Cruel Intentions, Blue Streak, The Skulls, Urban Legends: Final Cut, Saving Silverman, The Fast and the Furious, Soul Survivors, The Glass House, Not Another Teen Movie, Slackers, xXx, Sweet Home Alabama, 2 Fast 2 Furious, S.W.A.T, Out of Time, Torque, xXx: State of the Union, Stealth, The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift, Click, Gridiron Gang, Evan Almighty, I Am Legend, Vantage Point, Prom Night, The Bounty Hunter, The Green Hornet, Battle: Los Angeles, Fast Five, The Change-Up, 21 Jump Street, Total Recall, Jack the Giant Slayer, R.I.P.D., Fast & Furious 6, 22 Jump Street, and Furious 7. Moritz was nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Made for Television Movie for the Rat Pack in 1999.

Real Estate: In March 2020, Neal paid $9.7 million for a condo in Maui. They also own an 11,000 square-foot home in Brentwood, Los Angeles which is worth around $20 million.
